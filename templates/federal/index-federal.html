Thank You For Registering With The State Department of Human Intelligence

many studies and reports such as that written by Diac and another by Weschler, noted public concern over the collection of biometric information by governments. These stemmed from concerns of privacy, cyberattacks, and data-breaches.

This is where the 3rd party biometric database comes in!

The U.S. government has declared biometric signatures to be property of the entity responsible for the conversion of physical prints to alpha-numerical data. It is for this reason that a 3rd party entity, separate from the restaurants, banks, governments, etc. should be responsible for the distribution and creation of biometric signatures for widespread use amongst clients.

Inspired by Kerberos and TTP services

If the database for one partnering entity leaks, attackers won't have access to your original biometric. Instead, they'll have one of many hashed version of the signature, specifically salted for each entity that uses it.

To login, users will send a request for authentication to the application they have an account with, which will then be passed onto our service. From there, the user's biometric is compared to the template in our database and will be given access to the application upon a match. Similar to Kerberos' TGTs for creating session keys between the user and the web service.

What to do after a data-breach?

  • Use your email to request a new biometric signature.
  • Resubmit your fingerprint through the scanner.
  • A new salt will be given to the breached entity's database and be used to create your new hashed biometric signature.

The user will send their biometric signature, with our system converting that into a template to then be hashed and stored. We can then send this information to a Kerberos TTP for the creation of a Ticket Granting Ticket, containing the hashed biometric, session key, and expiration time.
In essence, this is a third party service which uses the concepts of Kerberos within biometric applications.

Users can use their emails within partnering sites to request for a new password. With that entity forwarding the request to us to create a new salted, hashed signature for the user.
Changing the salt upon request will help in preventing attackers from making use of the leaked information for account spoofing and injection attacks. Ensuring the database's continued safety after an attack along with the user's biometric safety and privacy.
